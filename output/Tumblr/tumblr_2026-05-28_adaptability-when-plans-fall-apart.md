# Adaptability Is the Skill That Saves You When Plans Fall Apart

Plans fall apart. That is not a failure of planning. That is how projects, careers, and organizations actually work.

The people who navigate disruption well are not the ones with the best contingency plans. They are the ones who stop defending the original plan faster than everyone else and redirect their energy toward what is actually possible now.

Adaptability is not flexibility about everything. It is clarity about what you are actually trying to achieve, separated from the specific path you assumed would get you there. When the path breaks, people who conflate the goal with the method lose both. People who hold the goal clearly can find another method.

**What adaptability looks like in practice:**

It looks like asking "given what is true now, what is the best move?" rather than "how do I fix what went wrong?" The second question is useful for retrospectives. The first is useful for moving forward.

It looks like being willing to communicate a changed plan openly rather than delaying the message to avoid the discomfort of admitting the original was wrong.

It looks like rebuilding the plan from the current state rather than patching the broken version of the old one.

It looks like treating the disruption as new information rather than as a problem with a specific right answer.

The people who get stuck longest are the ones trying to return to a situation that no longer exists. Adaptability starts with accepting that the new situation is the real one.
