# Pinterest Marketing Strategy

_Last updated: 2026-07-14_


## Goal and Metric

Drive consistent, compounding referral traffic from Pinterest to high-converting landing pages and product pages. Primary success metric: 10,000+ monthly outbound clicks from Pinterest within 9 months, with a content library structured so that at least 30% of monthly traffic comes from pins published more than 90 days prior.

---

## Core Principles

**1. Pinterest is a search engine, not a social feed.** Users arrive on Pinterest with intent: they are planning a purchase, a project, a life change, or an experience. The algorithm surfaces content based on keyword relevance, pin quality, and engagement signals, not recency alone. A pin that earns strong early engagement continues to surface in search results for 12 to 24 months.

**2. Keywords determine discoverability; visuals determine the click.** Pinterest SEO operates through pin titles, pin descriptions, board names, board descriptions, and alt text. Without deliberate keyword inclusion in all five of these fields, pins do not surface in search results regardless of visual quality. Keywords come first in the strategy; visual design comes second.

**3. Boards are the organizational architecture of the account.** A well-structured board system signals topical authority to Pinterest's algorithm and makes the account easier for users to navigate and follow. Each board represents one distinct topic area. Board names use natural language keywords, not clever branded names that no one searches.

**4. Seasonal content must publish 45 to 60 days before the occasion.** Pinterest users plan ahead. Content for a December holiday campaign performs best when published in October. Content for summer activity peaks in April and May, not June. Publishing seasonal content at the time of the event means arriving after the audience has already made decisions.

**5. Consistency outperforms volume spikes.** Pinning 5 to 10 pins daily from a consistent account history performs better than publishing 100 pins in one week followed by inactivity. Pinterest's smart feed penalizes irregular activity patterns and rewards accounts that demonstrate consistent, ongoing engagement with the platform.

## Strategy Architecture

**Step 1: Keyword research and board structure**
Before creating any content, conduct keyword research using Pinterest's own search bar. Type the primary topic and observe the suggested keyword bubbles that appear at the top of search results. These bubbles reveal the actual language Pinterest users use to describe the topic.

Build the board structure around keyword clusters:
- Create 15 to 25 boards, each representing a distinct subtopic or content category relevant to the brand
- Name each board with a 2 to 4 word keyword phrase (not a clever internal name)
- Write a 150 to 200 word board description that naturally includes 3 to 5 related keywords
- Organize boards so the most important ones appear in the first two rows of the profile

**Step 2: Pin format selection by goal**
Assign different pin formats to different conversion goals:
- **Static pins:** evergreen how-to content, product showcases, resource previews, tips and frameworks. These are the workhorses of a Pinterest strategy; they accumulate search rankings over time.
- **Video pins:** demonstration content, before-and-after reveals, process walkthroughs. Video pins earn more impressions in the home feed but are harder to repurpose from static content.
- **Idea Pins (multi-page format):** step-by-step tutorials, multi-part content, recipe or project formats. Idea Pins do not carry outbound links, making them better for audience building than direct traffic generation.
- **Product Pins (via Rich Pins or Shopping integration):** direct product listings with real-time pricing and availability data. These are the highest-converting format for ecommerce brands.

**Step 3: Pin anatomy for search performance**
Every static pin must include:
- A title of 40 to 100 characters with the primary keyword in the first 30 characters
- A description of 150 to 300 characters that includes the primary keyword plus 2 to 3 related keywords, written in natural sentences
- A destination URL with UTM parameters for traffic attribution
- Alt text that describes the image content and includes the primary keyword

**Step 4: Content calendar with seasonal offsets**
Build a rolling 90-day content calendar that accounts for seasonal lead time:
- Map all relevant seasonal moments for the brand's topic area (holidays, industry events, annual planning cycles, seasonal behavior changes)
- Assign content creation deadlines 75 to 90 days before each seasonal moment
- Assign publishing deadlines 45 to 60 days before each seasonal moment
- Maintain 30% of publishing slots for evergreen content that runs regardless of season

**Step 5: Rich Pins setup and maintenance**
Rich Pins pull metadata directly from the brand's website and automatically update when the source page changes. The three most useful Rich Pin types:
- Article Rich Pins (pull headline, author, and meta description from blog posts)
- Product Rich Pins (pull price, availability, and product description from product pages)

Enable Rich Pins through Pinterest's validation tool and keep the corresponding meta tags on the website current. Outdated or broken Rich Pins lose search ranking and click-through rates.

## Content Pillar Framework

**Pillar 1: Educational how-to content**
Step-by-step guides, tutorials, checklists, and frameworks in static pin format. These generate the highest volume of long-tail search impressions and consistently earn saves, which are the primary engagement signal Pinterest uses to determine ranking.

**Pillar 2: Curated resource collections**
Boards and pins that compile the best resources, tools, products, or ideas on a specific topic. These perform well as standalone pins and earn links and repins from others who find the compilation useful.

**Pillar 3: Inspirational content with clear attribution**
Aspiration-forward content that shows what is possible. Must include the brand's URL and clear visual branding so that repins (which can travel far from the original account) carry brand attribution.

**Pillar 4: Seasonal planning content**
Content explicitly designed for seasonal searches, published on the 45-to-60-day lead schedule. Seasonal content earns disproportionately high impressions during peak planning windows.

**Pillar 5: Product or service context content**
Pins that show how the product or service fits into a broader context or project. Not direct product advertising but lifestyle-adjacent or use-case content that naturally leads to a product discovery moment.

## Cadence

- Publish 10 to 15 new pins per day during the build phase (first 6 months), using a scheduling tool to distribute pins evenly throughout the day
- Pin original content (created by the brand) at a ratio of at least 60% original to 40% curated/repinned from other accounts
- Publish at least 2 new boards per month during the first 6 months to expand topical coverage
- Review and update board descriptions every 90 days as keyword research reveals new search patterns
- After 6 months, reduce to 5 to 10 pins per day while maintaining seasonal publishing schedule

## Distribution and Amplification

Pinterest content does not benefit from cross-platform amplification the way that blog or video content does. The distribution loop for Pinterest is internal: content that earns saves and engagement within Pinterest surfaces more widely in search results and the home feed.

External amplification strategies that do work:
- Add "Save to Pinterest" buttons to all blog posts, product pages, and landing pages to enable organic pinning from the brand's own website
- Include one Pinterest-optimized image in every blog post (vertical format, 2:3 ratio, with overlay text that works without reading the article) to make the blog post easily pinnable
- Include Pinterest in the email newsletter's social follow links and periodically feature a specific board or pin collection as a newsletter content item

## Failure Modes

1. **Treating Pinterest like Instagram.** Instagram rewards aesthetic consistency, high-production visual style, and community engagement in comments. Pinterest rewards keyword density, practical utility, and long-term search visibility. Pins designed for Instagram aesthetics often underperform on Pinterest because they lack text overlays, keyword-rich descriptions, and direct-response clarity.

2. **Posting without keywords.** Pins with beautiful images but no keyword-optimized titles, descriptions, or alt text are invisible in search. Pinterest cannot infer what a pin is about from the image alone. Every pin title and description is a search indexing opportunity.

3. **No board structure or poorly named boards.** An account with 3 generic boards or boards named after internal brand campaigns rather than actual search terms cannot develop topical authority. Boards named "Our Products" or "Campaign 2026" earn zero search visibility compared to boards named with natural-language keywords.

4. **Irregular pinning cadence.** Publishing 50 pins in one week and then nothing for three weeks triggers Pinterest's algorithm to deprioritize the account's content. Consistent daily publishing is more important than high-volume bursts.

5. **Publishing seasonal content at the wrong time.** A Christmas gift guide published on December 15 reaches an audience that has already completed most of its shopping. Pinterest seasonal windows require publishing at least 6 weeks early, and ideally 8 to 10 weeks early for high-competition seasonal moments.

## Recommended Tools

- **Pinterest Analytics** (native; tracks impressions, saves, and outbound clicks by pin and board; essential for identifying which content drives actual traffic)
- **Tailwind** (Pinterest-native scheduling and analytics platform; includes SmartSchedule for optimal posting times, Communities for group pinning, and performance analytics beyond what Pinterest's native tool provides)
- **Canva** (template creation for branded Pinterest graphics; use Canva's Pinterest-specific templates which default to the correct 2:3 aspect ratio and include text overlay options optimized for Pinterest's visual format)
- **Pinterest Trends** (free native tool that shows search volume trends for specific topics over time; use it to validate seasonal publishing timing before building the content calendar)
- **Semrush or Ahrefs** (for identifying which blog posts on the brand's website already drive organic traffic, so those posts get Pinterest-optimized images created and pinned first to compound existing search performance)

## Adaptation for Your Brand

Pinterest's audience skews toward home improvement, fashion, food, wellness, DIY, events, travel, and personal finance. B2B brands can succeed on Pinterest but require a deliberate pivot from product marketing to practical education content. A B2B software company earns Pinterest traffic not by pinning product screenshots but by pinning workflow frameworks, checklists, and process diagrams that are genuinely useful to the professionals who would use the software.

Determine the content format fit before investing in Pinterest. If the brand produces content that can be translated into visually clear, scannable, pinnable formats (checklists, step-by-step visuals, comparison charts, infographic-style summaries), Pinterest will compound that content's reach significantly. If the brand's content is primarily long-form text, video, or audio with no visual translation layer, Pinterest requires a content adaptation investment before the channel becomes viable.

The brand's domain should be claimed and verified in Pinterest account settings before the first pin is published. A verified domain adds a badge to pins and enables more robust analytics tracking.
