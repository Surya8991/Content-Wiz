# Link Acquisition Strategy

_Last updated: 2026-07-14_


## Goal and Metric

Build a topically relevant, editorially earned backlink profile that strengthens domain authority and supports organic ranking for the brand's primary keyword clusters. Primary success metric: acquire 20 to 30 new referring domains per month with a Domain Rating of 40+ and topical relevance to the brand's subject area, sustained over 12 months.

---

## Core Principles

**1. Topical relevance outweighs raw domain authority.** A link from a DR 45 website that specifically covers the brand's topic area is more valuable for ranking purposes than a DR 75 link from an unrelated niche. Google's link evaluation in 2026 weights the semantic relationship between the linking page's topic and the linked page's topic heavily. The quality filter starts with: is this site covering the same topic the brand covers?

**2. Links are earned through assets, not through asking.** Cold outreach that lacks a specific, genuinely useful asset to offer produces declining response rates and wastes the budget. Link building works when there is something worth linking to: original data, a reference-quality guide, a tool, or a resource that is genuinely better than what exists. Create the asset first; the outreach is just awareness distribution.

**3. Anchor text diversity is a ranking signal, not just a hygiene rule.** A backlink profile dominated by exact-match keyword anchor text is a spam signal. A natural backlink profile has branded anchors (the company name), naked URL anchors, partial-match anchors, and generic anchors ("click here," "this resource") in rough proportion to how real writers naturally link to things. Optimize for this distribution, not for maximum keyword-rich anchors.

**4. A disavow strategy is ongoing maintenance, not a one-time cleanup.** Spam, negative SEO attacks, and link farms will accumulate over time. Monitoring the backlink profile quarterly and maintaining a disavow file prevents low-quality links from dragging down domain authority and signals to Google that the brand actively manages its link profile.

**5. Relationships compound; transactional outreach does not.** A journalist who receives a useful data point from the brand once and gets a follow-up with equally useful data becomes a reliable coverage source. A brand that sends one cold pitch and never follows up with value builds no relationship and generates no repeat coverage. Link building is a relationship business with SEO measurement on top.

## Strategy Architecture

**Step 1: Linkable asset audit and creation**
Before any outreach begins, audit existing content for assets that are genuinely worth linking to:
- Original research reports with specific statistics
- Comprehensive reference guides that are more complete than competitors
- Free tools (calculators, templates, assessments, generators)
- Data visualizations or original datasets
- Case studies with specific, named results

If none of these exist, the first investment is creating them. Attempting to build links to thin content, product pages, or blog posts that duplicate what competitors have already published produces a response rate approaching zero.

**Step 2: Prospecting methods**

Broken link building:
- Use Ahrefs or Screaming Frog to identify pages in the brand's niche that have 10+ external backlinks but return 404 errors
- The outreach pitch: "Your article links to [resource], which now returns a 404. We have a resource that covers the same topic: [link]. Happy to share if it would be a useful replacement."
- This method works because the pitch solves the linker's problem (a broken link on their page), not the brand's problem

Resource page outreach:
- Use search operators to find resource pages in the niche: `intitle:"resources" "training" site:edu`, `inurl:resources + [topic keyword]`
- Resource pages curate links to valuable external resources; the pitch is that the brand's asset belongs on this list
- This method has a response rate of 5 to 15% with a well-targeted list and a specific, relevant asset

Competitor backlink gap analysis:
- Use Ahrefs or Semrush to identify sites that link to 2+ competitors in the brand's category but do not link to the brand
- These sites have demonstrated willingness to link to the brand's category; the gap represents an outreach opportunity
- The pitch angle: the brand's asset on the relevant topic is more current, more comprehensive, or differently positioned than what they currently link to

HARO and Featured (journalist sourcing platforms):
- Monitor daily queries on Help a Reporter Out, Featured, and Qwoted for requests in the brand's topic area
- Provide specific, quotable expert commentary with real data and named sources within 2 hours of a request going live (speed determines whether the journalist uses the brand's response)
- Each media placement that results from this method earns a high-authority editorial link with no outreach cost

Digital PR (proactive story pitching):
- Identify a data story from the brand's own research that is newsworthy to journalists covering the relevant beat
- Pitch the story with the data angle first, not the brand story
- Successful digital PR campaigns earn 10 to 50+ editorial links from a single data release

**Step 3: Outreach cadence**
Cold outreach sequence for resource page and broken link building:
- Email 1: personalized pitch with specific reason the brand's asset belongs on their page (reference a specific post or section of theirs to demonstrate familiarity)
- Follow-up 1 (5 business days later): a short reply to the original email that adds one additional relevant detail or data point ("I also thought this might be useful: [relevant stat]")
- Follow-up 2 (7 business days after follow-up 1): final brief note that provides an easy out ("No worries if it's not the right fit, just want to make sure this reached you") and closes the sequence

Sending a third follow-up beyond this sequence in the absence of any reply damages deliverability and produces diminishing returns.

**Timing rules:**
- Send outreach emails Tuesday through Thursday, 8am to 10am recipient time zone
- Avoid sending on Mondays (inbox clearing day) and Fridays (weekend transition)
- Do not send outreach during major industry events or holidays when journalists and publishers are unavailable

## Anchor Text Distribution Framework

Target distribution for a natural backlink profile:
- Branded anchors (company name, product name): 40 to 50% of new links
- Naked URL anchors (just the URL): 15 to 20%
- Partial-match keyword anchors (topic word but not exact target keyword): 15 to 20%
- Generic anchors ("here," "this article," "click here," "more info"): 10 to 15%
- Exact-match keyword anchors (the target keyword verbatim): 5% maximum

If the existing backlink profile has more than 15% exact-match keyword anchors, pause exact-match outreach entirely until the distribution normalizes through new link acquisition.

---

## What Makes a Backlink Worth Pursuing

A backlink is worth the outreach investment if it meets all four criteria:
1. The linking domain has a Domain Rating of 40 or higher (Ahrefs DR) or a Domain Authority of 40+ (Moz DA)
2. The linking page is topically relevant to the brand's content area (not just a high-DR domain in an unrelated niche)
3. The linking domain receives meaningful organic traffic (check with Ahrefs Site Explorer; aim for sites with 1,000+ monthly organic visits)
4. The link would be placed editorially within body content, not in a footer, sidebar, or author bio

Links that do not meet all four criteria are not worth pursuing. A link from a DR 20 blog with no traffic in an unrelated niche provides no meaningful SEO value and may be a net negative.

## Disavow File Maintenance

Review the disavow file quarterly:
- Use Ahrefs or Google Search Console to audit all referring domains
- Flag links from link farms, private blog networks, unrelated directories, foreign-language spam sites, and any domain with a spam score above 60
- Submit the updated disavow file to Google Search Console after each quarterly review
- Do not disavow links from real editorial sites, even if the content is negative or critical; disavow is for spam, not for coverage the brand dislikes

---

## Cadence

- Dedicate 10 to 20 hours per month to outreach during the build phase
- Target 30 to 50 outreach contacts per week during active prospecting phases (with personalized pitches, not mass-blast templates)
- Review the backlink profile with Ahrefs or Semrush monthly to track new referring domains, lost links, and anchor text distribution
- Conduct a full disavow audit quarterly

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Mass-blast outreach.** Sending the same generic email to 500 prospects without personalization produces response rates below 1% and risks the sending domain being flagged as spam by email providers. Personalized outreach to 30 well-targeted contacts produces more placements than mass outreach to 500 untargeted ones.

2. **Exact-match anchor text overuse.** Systematically acquiring links with the exact target keyword as the anchor text is a link scheme pattern that triggers Google's spam classifier. A backlink profile with 30%+ exact-match anchors is a manual penalty risk regardless of the individual link quality.

3. **Buying links.** Paid links violate Google's spam policies and produce short-term ranking gains that collapse on any core update. The disavow cleanup required after a paid link penalty costs more than the original link-buying investment.

4. **Ignoring topical relevance.** Acquiring 50 links from unrelated niches because the DR is attractive fails the core premise of link building in 2026. Topically irrelevant links do not contribute meaningfully to ranking in the brand's topic area and can signal manipulative behavior.

5. **No follow-up system.** Sending one outreach email and not following up loses 50 to 70% of potential responses. Most positive outreach replies come from the first or second follow-up, not the initial pitch. A CRM or outreach tool that tracks reply status and triggers follow-ups automatically is not optional for teams doing more than 20 contacts per week.

6. **Building links without building linkable assets first.** Outreach without a genuinely useful asset to offer produces the kind of response a cold pitch without value has always produced: almost none.

## Recommended Tools

- **Ahrefs** (primary tool for competitor backlink gap analysis, broken link identification, referring domain monitoring, and anchor text distribution tracking; the most comprehensive link intelligence platform available in 2026)
- **Semrush Link Building Tool** (combines outreach tracking with prospect discovery; allows prioritizing outreach contacts by authority and relevance within a single workflow)
- **Hunter.io** (email finder for identifying journalist and editor email addresses for outreach; the Chrome extension allows finding contact details while browsing the target site directly)
- **Mailshake or Pitchbox** (outreach automation platforms that allow personalized email sequences at scale, reply detection, and outreach performance tracking without requiring manual follow-up management)
- **Google Search Console** (free; the backlink report shows all Google-confirmed links, and the disavow file submission tool is only accessible here)

---

## Adaptation for Your Brand

The link building tactics that perform best depend on the brand's available content assets. If the brand has original data or research, digital PR and HARO are the highest-leverage starting points. If the brand has comprehensive educational content, broken link building and resource page outreach produce the most volume. If the brand's content is primarily product-focused with limited educational depth, the first step is creating linkable assets before investing in outreach.

B2B brands in complex, specialized industries often have an advantage in link building because the number of relevant prospects is smaller but the authority of niche industry publications is highly valuable. A single link from the definitive industry trade publication in a B2B niche often outperforms 10 links from general business blogs.

Link building results are measured in months, not weeks. A newly launched domain building links from scratch should not expect meaningful ranking movements in the first 3 months. Set stakeholder expectations accordingly and track leading indicators (referring domain growth, outreach response rate) before expecting lagging indicators (ranking movement, traffic growth) to materialize.
