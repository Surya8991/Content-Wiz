# Substack Newsletter and Notes Strategy

_Last updated: 2026-07-13_

## Goal and Metric

Build a Substack publication that uses Notes as a daily discovery engine and the newsletter as the conversion asset that turns subscribers into an owned, high-engagement email list. Target: grow to 1,000 free subscribers within 90 days using daily Notes, achieve a 45%+ open rate on newsletter issues (the platform average, versus roughly 20% for the email industry overall), and establish Recommendations as a passive cross-audience growth loop within the first two weeks.

---

## Core Principles

**1. The conversion unit is an email address, not a follower.** Substack Notes is the only major social platform whose algorithm is explicitly engineered to convert engagement into owned email subscribers. Every "restack" (the Substack equivalent of a repost) includes a one-click subscribe button to the original writer's newsletter. On Instagram, a share sends someone deeper into Instagram. On Substack, a restack sends someone out of the feed and into the subscriber list. Followers on Substack are nearly worthless as a business asset; only email subscribers compound into revenue. A Notes strategy that drives 200 followers but 5 subscribers is a failure. A strategy that drives 50 followers and 40 subscribers is a success.

**2. Restacks from accounts with overlapping audiences are the primary algorithmic signal.** Substack rebuilt its recommendation system in 2025 from static two-tower models to sequential learning architectures. The algorithm asks one question at the point of serving a Note: "Will this reader eventually pay for this writer's work?" It does not care how long you scroll or how many likes a Note receives. The three signals the algorithm rewards in order of weight: restacks from accounts with overlapping audiences, replies from established accounts (a reply from someone with 10,000 subscribers carries far more weight than 50 likes from random accounts), and subscriber-conversion rate per impression (Notes that drive subscribe clicks get more distribution than Notes that just collect likes).

**3. Daily Notes for 90 days is the entry cost.** In a three-month window in 2025, 32 million new subscriptions were sourced entirely from within the Substack app, with Notes as the primary driver. Notes posters who publish daily grow up to 70% of their subscribers from Notes alone (Substack internal data). The algorithm needs time to learn the audience shape. Creators who quit after 2 to 3 weeks almost universally report no results. Those who publish daily past day 45 almost universally report compounding growth. Frequency is the entry ticket; quality is the growth lever.

**4. Recommendations are the highest-ROI growth mechanism on the platform, and most creators ignore it.** The Recommendations feature creates a built-in cross-promotion loop: recommend 8 to 12 Substacks in the same niche, and most will reciprocate. This requires zero ongoing effort after a 10-minute setup and generates passive subscriber flow from every reciprocating publication's new subscriber onboarding. Creators who ignore Recommendations leave 30 to 50% of organic growth uncaptured, based on multiple 2025 case studies. Activate this feature in the first week, not after reaching some subscriber threshold.

**5. External links in Notes suppress reach; the only acceptable link placement is in comments.** Notes with external links in the body receive significantly reduced algorithmic distribution. Substack's algorithm deprioritizes any Note that attempts to send traffic outside the platform. The one acceptable link placement is in the first comment below the Note, or in the pinned welcome Note on the profile. This is counterintuitive for marketers trained to drive traffic to external destinations in every piece of content. On Substack, the goal is to earn the subscribe action within the platform; external links interrupt that path.

## Strategy Architecture

**Step 1: Set up the publication and activate Recommendations before posting the first Note**
Before the first piece of content goes live, complete the technical setup: profile photo, bio that communicates the niche in one sentence, welcome Note pinned to the profile (the one place where an external link is acceptable, used to direct new profile visitors to subscribe), and Recommendations activated with 8 to 12 Substacks in the target niche. The Recommendations setup takes 10 minutes and begins generating passive subscriber flow from every reciprocating publication's new subscriber onboarding immediately. Starting without Recommendations means leaving the highest-ROI passive growth loop inactive for weeks or months.

**Step 2: Post 1 to 3 Notes per day using the four proven formats**
Frequency is the algorithm's entry cost. Daily posting compounds because each Note trains the algorithm on what the audience looks like. Four formats work consistently: contrarian take ("Everyone says X. Here is exactly why X is wrong in 2025"), vulnerable confession ("I earned zero from my newsletter for 14 months. Here is the specific mistake"), generosity Note (publicly recommend 5 to 10 writers in the niche by @-handle with a one-line reason for each), and behind-the-scenes process (screenshot analytics, drafts, reading lists, or morning routines). The generosity Note consistently produces the highest restack rates of any content type, driving subscriber spikes of 200 to 400 new emails in 24 to 48 hours after a large writer restacks it.

**Step 3: Restack 5 to 10 Notes from larger accounts daily with a quote-comment**
Restacking with a quote-comment sends a signal that audiences overlap. When a larger writer restacks the restack, that amplification is worth more than 10 original posts from a small account. In late 2025, Substack began treating restacks as fresh content, giving them a second distribution window 1 to 2 days after the original post. This means a thoughtful restack with a genuine commentary adds a second reach event without requiring new content creation. Target writers with 3,000 to 30,000 subscribers in the niche: large enough to amplify meaningfully, small enough to notice and reciprocate.

**Step 4: Convert winning Notes into newsletter issues within 7 days**
Any Note that crosses 100 likes is a confirmed interest signal from an audience that is already warm. Turn it into a full newsletter issue while the audience is warm and traffic is still building. This is the flywheel connection between Notes and the newsletter: Notes function as a real-time market research tool that identifies which angles, topics, and framings resonate before investing in a full-length issue. Lenny Rachitsky uses this approach systematically, excerpting charts and quotes from newsletter issues into standalone Notes, then developing the Notes that generate the most engagement into deeper issues or courses.

**Step 5: Structure the welcome sequence to convert free subscribers to paid**
Free subscribers who discover the publication through Notes arrive with a specific interest signal (the Note that earned their subscribe). The welcome sequence should acknowledge that context: "You subscribed after reading [subject area]—here are the 3 most useful things I have written on that topic." A welcome sequence that delivers immediate value within the first 48 hours converts free subscribers to paid at a materially higher rate than a generic welcome email. B2B newsletter operators have reported paid conversion rates of 2 to 4% from Note readers who arrive via restack traffic, compared to under 0.5% from cold ad traffic to the same offer.

**Step 6: Measure subscribers per Note, not likes, as the primary performance metric**
The algorithmic goal is subscriber conversion, not engagement validation. A 50-like Note that drives 30 subscribers beats a 500-like Note that drives 5 subscribers in every commercially relevant dimension. Set up a tracking system that connects the publication date of each Note to the daily subscriber acquisition count in Substack analytics. Notes published on days with above-average subscriber gains reveal which formats, topics, and framings are driving the conversion signal the algorithm actually rewards.

## Content Pillars

**Contrarian Takes: Niche-Specific Opinions That Generate Restacks**
Short Notes (under 300 words) that challenge a widely accepted position in the publication's topic area with enough specificity to be arguable. The claim must be verifiable or defensible, and the author must be prepared to engage substantively in replies. This format generates the highest restack rates from writers in the same niche who find the take useful to their own audiences, triggering the overlapping-audience amplification that the algorithm rewards most heavily.

**Generosity Notes: Weekly Public Recommendations of Other Writers**
A weekly Note that publicly recommends 5 to 10 writers in the niche by @-handle with one specific reason for each recommendation. This triggers the social norm of reciprocal resharing: writers who are recommended publicly feel compelled to restack the recommendation to their audiences, which is often 10 to 50 times larger than the recommending account's. One well-targeted generosity Note can reach 50,000 new readers at zero cost. Post this format on the same day each week to establish reader expectation.

**Behind-the-Scenes Process: Transparency About Real Numbers and Decisions**
Notes that share real analytics screenshots, subscriber milestones, revenue data, failed experiments, or unexpected outcomes from running the publication. This format generates the highest follow rates from readers who want to learn from the writer's specific experience, not just their opinions. The "Cubicle Escape" creator documented on their public Substack that daily Notes posting drove consistent growth of 10 or more new subscribers per day for over a year, building 15,000 subscribers and $100K in revenue primarily through Notes. Transparency about the process makes the success credible and replicable for the reader.

**Newsletter Excerpts: Converting Issue Content to Notes Distribution**
Excerpts from newsletter issues posted as standalone Notes, with a subscribe prompt in the first comment below the Note (not in the body). This doubles the surface area of each newsletter issue without adding writing time. Lenny's Newsletter crossed 1 million subscribers using this approach: one newsletter issue becomes six to eight Notes posted across 7 to 10 days, each targeting a different hook into the same underlying content. Notes that drive above-average subscribers from this format become candidates for full-length issue expansion.

## Cadence

Post 1 to 3 Notes per day, 7 days per week, for the first 90 days without interruption. The algorithm needs 30 to 45 days to learn the audience shape; any gap in the daily posting habit during this period resets the learning trajectory. After day 90, maintain daily posting but reduce to 1 to 2 Notes per day as the publication grows and newsletter production takes more time.

Newsletter cadence: weekly or bi-weekly. Substack email open rates average over 45%, versus roughly 20% for the email industry overall. This open rate advantage is maintained through consistent publishing schedules. An unpredictable publishing cadence trains subscribers to deprioritize emails from the publication.

Review cycle: weekly audit of subscribers-per-Note for all Notes published in the prior 7 days. Monthly review of Recommendations reciprocation: which Substacks have sent the most subscribers, and which deserve priority in the weekly generosity Note. Quarterly review of Notes-to-subscriber conversion rate to identify format drift.

**Publishing Volume**

- Notes: 1 to 3 per day (7 days per week for first 90 days)
- Restacks with quote-comment: 5 to 10 per day
- Newsletter issues: 1 per week (or bi-weekly at scale)
- Generosity Note: 1 per week
- Winning Notes converted to newsletter issues: 1 to 2 per month (based on those crossing 100 likes)

## Distribution & Amplification

1. **Activate Recommendations in week 1 and add to them monthly.** Go to Settings, find Recommendations, and add 8 to 12 Substacks in the niche. Most will reciprocate. Add 2 to 3 new Recommendations per month as the network grows. This is the platform's built-in cross-promotion loop and it requires zero ongoing effort after setup. Multiple 2025 case studies confirm that creators who ignore Recommendations leave 30 to 50% of organic growth uncaptured.

2. **Use the generosity Note as the primary external amplification lever.** One weekly generosity Note that publicly recommends 5 to 10 writers by @-handle, with a specific one-line reason for each recommendation, is the most reliable zero-cost amplification mechanism on Substack. When a writer with 30,000 subscribers restacks a generosity Note, that single restack can drive 200 to 400 new subscriber emails in 48 hours. Target writers who are slightly larger than the current publication size (5x to 20x larger), not the very largest accounts whose audiences are unlikely to overlap.

3. **Cross-promote the Substack newsletter in other owned channels without violating the no-external-links-in-Notes rule.** Drive traffic to the Substack publication from LinkedIn posts, X/Twitter threads, email signatures, and podcast appearances. The no-external-links rule applies only to Notes inside Substack; external promotion from other platforms to the Substack subscription page is unlimited and generates subscribers who arrive with higher intent than algorithmic discovery.

4. **Leverage the fediverse integration for reach beyond the Substack audience.** Substack's ActivityPub integration connects the platform to the broader fediverse. Notes and publications are increasingly visible on Mastodon and other ActivityPub platforms. This does not require any additional action from the creator but represents organic reach into communities where email newsletter readers are concentrated.

5. **Convert top-performing Notes into LinkedIn and X posts to drive inbound subscriber traffic.** Notes that generate above-average engagement on Substack can be repurposed to LinkedIn (with a link to the Substack subscribe page in the comments, not the post body) and X/Twitter. This external distribution drives profile visits from audiences who are already in the niche and have seen evidence of the publication's content quality before arriving at the subscribe page.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**1. Treating Notes like Twitter.** Threading, ratio-baiting, and engagement-farming hooks underperform on Substack because the algorithm does not reward dwell time or like volume. It rewards restacks from accounts whose audiences overlap with the publication's. Content that generates likes without generating restacks from niche-relevant accounts earns no algorithmic distribution to new potential subscribers.

**2. Putting external links inside Notes.** External links suppress reach significantly. The one acceptable link placement is in the first comment below the Note, or in the pinned welcome Note on the profile. Every external link in a Note body is a reach penalty. This is the most counterintuitive rule for marketers trained to drive traffic in every piece of content.

**3. Posting only on newsletter publish days.** Substack creators who stalled after the algorithm update in late 2024 were almost all weekly-or-less Note posters. The algorithm needs daily signal to learn the audience shape. Publishing Notes only when the newsletter goes out means going dark for 6 days out of 7, which the algorithm interprets as inactivity and reduces distribution accordingly.

**4. Skipping Recommendations.** Creators who ignore Recommendations leave 30 to 50% of organic growth uncaptured. It takes 10 minutes to set up and works passively from that point forward. There is no other single action available on Substack that generates passive subscriber flow without ongoing effort.

**5. Giving up before day 45.** The algorithm needs time to learn the audience shape. Creators who quit after 2 to 3 weeks almost universally report no results. Those who publish daily past day 45 almost universally report compounding growth. 90 days is the evaluation horizon; 14 days is noise.

## Recommended Tools

- **Substack native platform (on.substack.com):** Creator resources, analytics, Recommendations management, and Notes scheduling; the primary operating environment for everything in this strategy.
- **Substack Analytics:** Track daily subscriber acquisition by Note published; the core measurement tool for subscribers-per-Note and the format performance audit.
- **Backlinko Substack Statistics:** Platform-level growth data (50M active subscriptions, 5M paid subscriptions, $600M paid out to creators in 2025); use to contextualize investment decisions and communicate platform scale to stakeholders.
- **Typefully or Buffer:** Draft, schedule, and publish Notes at consistent times; consistency of posting time reduces friction for daily habit and allows A/B testing of time-of-day effects on subscriber conversion.
- **Beehiiv or ConvertKit (for comparison):** Alternative newsletter platforms with better analytics than Substack; evaluate if the publication grows beyond the stage where Substack's native analytics are sufficient for optimization decisions.

## Adaptation for Your Brand

Substack strategy differs primarily by whether the publication is building an audience from zero or converting an existing audience from another platform. For zero-to-one publications, the Notes strategy is the primary growth engine: daily posting, daily restacking, weekly generosity Notes, and immediate Recommendations activation. The newsletter itself serves as the conversion and monetization asset, not the discovery asset.

For publications migrating from an existing email list (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.), the immediate priority is importing the existing list and activating Recommendations before publishing the first Note. The imported subscribers provide a day-1 social proof signal (subscriber count) that makes restacks from larger accounts more likely, because writers with large audiences are more willing to recommend publications that already have an established reader base.

For B2B brands using Substack as a content marketing channel rather than a monetized media property, the goal metric shifts from paid subscription revenue to free subscriber acquisition as a top-of-funnel lead source. B2B newsletter operators have reported paid conversion rates of 2 to 4% from Note readers who arrive via restack traffic, compared to under 0.5% from cold ad traffic to the same offer. Even without a paid tier, the quality of email subscribers acquired through Substack Notes justifies the channel relative to paid acquisition alternatives.

Brands considering Substack for corporate publishing should evaluate the personal-account architecture: Notes from named individuals at the company (founders, executives, practitioners) generate more restacks and higher subscriber conversion than Notes from brand handles. This is the same dynamic documented on Threads and LinkedIn: personal authority drives algorithmic distribution on text-first platforms more reliably than brand authority.
