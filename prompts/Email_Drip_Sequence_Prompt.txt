============================================================
EMAIL DRIP SEQUENCE PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Create a [NUMBER e.g. 5]-email drip sequence for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], for the campaign "[CAMPAIGN GOAL e.g. Nurturing leads who downloaded a guide / Onboarding new users / Post-webinar follow-up]". For each email provide: the send timing (e.g. Day 0, Day 2, Day 5), a subject line, preview text, and email body. Each email should build on the previous one with a clear narrative arc: introduce value, educate, build trust, handle objections, and close with a CTA. Write in a conversational B2B tone targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Do not use em dashes.

Landing Page / Resource URL: [INSERT URL]
Lead Magnet or Trigger (if any): [e.g. Downloaded the L&D Guide / Attended Webinar]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Email No. | Send Timing | Subject Line | Preview Text | Email Body

Save to output/Email_Drip/

------------------------------------------------------------
SEQUENCE STRUCTURE (DEFAULT 5-EMAIL ARC)
------------------------------------------------------------

Email 1 (Day 0)   - Welcome and deliver immediate value
Email 2 (Day 2)   - Educate with a key insight or resource
Email 3 (Day 5)   - Social proof or case study
Email 4 (Day 8)   - Handle a common objection
Email 5 (Day 12)  - Final CTA with urgency or clear next step

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Subject line under 60 characters
- Preview text under 100 characters
- Each email body 100-150 words
- Each email has one clear CTA
- Narrative arc must flow logically across all emails
- Any stat or data point cited (e.g. in the social proof or case study email) must name a source, organization, and year. Never invent a plausible-sounding citation
- Never reuse the identical subject line or CTA phrasing across multiple sequences generated in the same session - if you are generating more than one drip sequence in a session, no two sequences may share a subject line or CTA phrasing
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- Output saved to output/Email_Drip/
