============================================================
EMAIL DRIP SEQUENCE PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Create a [NUMBER e.g. 5]-email drip sequence for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], for the campaign "[CAMPAIGN GOAL e.g. Nurturing leads who downloaded a guide / Onboarding new users / Post-webinar follow-up]". For each email provide: the send timing (e.g. Day 0, Day 2, Day 5), a subject line, preview text, and email body. Each email should build on the previous one with a clear narrative arc: introduce value, educate, build trust, handle objections, and close with a CTA. Write in a conversational tone matching the brand's market register per _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt rule 3 (default: conversational B2B) targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Do not use em dashes.

Landing Page / Resource URL: [INSERT URL]
Lead Magnet or Trigger (if any): [e.g. Downloaded the L&D Guide / Attended Webinar]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B / conversational B2B / first-person / brand account — default: professional B2B]
Sender Persona: [individual / brand — first-person 'I' / 'we' — default: individual]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Email No. | Send Timing | Subject Line | Preview Text | Email Body

Field encoding: wrap any field containing commas or line breaks in double quotes.
Email Body sub-elements: (1) subject line hook restated as opener, (2) core value or insight, (3) CTA — all three required in every email.
Subject Line max 60 characters; Preview Text max 90 characters.

Save to output/Email_Drip/

------------------------------------------------------------
SEQUENCE STRUCTURE (DEFAULT 5-EMAIL ARC)
------------------------------------------------------------

Email 1 (Day 0)   - Welcome and deliver immediate value
Email 2 (Day 2)   - Educate with a key insight or resource
Email 3 (Day 5)   - Social proof or case study
Email 4 (Day 8)   - Handle a common objection
Email 5 (Day 12)  - Final CTA with urgency or clear next step

------------------------------------------------------------
LIFECYCLE STAGE MAPPING
------------------------------------------------------------

Select the sequence type that matches the subscriber's lifecycle stage.

STAGE 1 — WELCOME / ONBOARDING
Trigger: New subscriber or new customer sign-up (Day 0)
Length: 3–5 emails over 7–10 days
Primary goal: Deliver the promised value and establish the sender relationship
Tone: Warm, personal, low-friction
Most important email: Email 2 — this is the first email the subscriber chose to open (Email 1 is expected). Email 2 proves the subscription was worth it.

STAGE 2 — ACTIVATION
Trigger: User has not taken the key action within 7–14 days of sign-up
Length: 2–3 emails over 7 days
Primary goal: Remove friction between intent and first action
Tone: Helpful, specific, one-CTA-only
Most important email: Email 1 — name the exact action they haven't taken and offer to help them take it now.

STAGE 3 — RETENTION / NURTURE
Trigger: Active user, periodic cadence (weekly or bi-weekly)
Length: Ongoing, no fixed end
Primary goal: Deliver consistent value that reinforces the subscriber's decision to stay
Tone: Conversational, educational, low-sell
Most important email: Whichever email in the month contains original data or a named framework — this is the one that gets forwarded.

STAGE 4 — WIN-BACK
Trigger: No email open or click in 60–90 days
Length: 3 emails, sent over 2 weeks, then suppress
Primary goal: Re-establish relevance before permanent list removal
Tone: Direct, no-nonsense, acknowledge the silence
Most important email: Email 1 — subject line must break the pattern. "Still want in?" outperforms "We miss you" every time.

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Subject line under 60 characters
- Preview text under 100 characters
- Each email body 100-150 words
- Each email must have exactly one CTA: imperative verb, specific action, 10–20 words. Example: 'Download the behavior-change audit checklist — link below.' A vague CTA ('learn more', 'check it out') fails this test.
- Narrative arc must flow logically across all emails
- Any stat or data point cited (e.g. in the social proof or case study email) must name a source, organization, and year. Never invent a plausible-sounding citation
- Never reuse the identical subject line or CTA phrasing across multiple sequences generated in the same session - if you are generating more than one drip sequence in a session, no two sequences may share a subject line or CTA phrasing
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- SELF-CHECK: After drafting each email, count words. Target: 100–150 per email body. If over 150, cut the body to one core point before outputting.
- LIFECYCLE STAGE: Identify the subscriber's lifecycle stage before selecting the sequence format. A win-back sequence sent to a new subscriber, or a welcome sequence sent to a churned user, signals poor segmentation and reduces deliverability scores.
- Output saved to output/Email_Drip/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. EMAIL BODY OVER 150 WORDS: Drip emails are read on mobile in under 30 seconds. An email body over 150 words loses the reader before the CTA. Cut to one core point per email.

2. MORE THAN ONE CTA IN A SINGLE EMAIL: Multiple CTAs split attention and reduce click-through. Every email must close with exactly one CTA — the most important action for that stage of the sequence.

3. SUBJECT LINE OVER 60 CHARACTERS: Subject lines beyond 60 characters are truncated on most email clients. Count characters before outputting and trim if needed.

4. EMAIL 1 ASKING FOR A SALE BEFORE DELIVERING VALUE: The first email must deliver the promised lead magnet or resource. Asking for a purchase or demo in Email 1 breaks trust with a cold-to-warm lead.

5. PREVIEW TEXT THAT REPEATS THE SUBJECT LINE WORD-FOR-WORD: Preview text is a second opportunity to earn the open. Repeating the subject line wastes that slot. Write preview text that adds context or a reason to open — different words, different angle.
