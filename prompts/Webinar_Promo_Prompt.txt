============================================================
WEBINAR PROMO COPY PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Create promotional copy for a webinar hosted by [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], titled "[WEBINAR TITLE]". Generate the following assets:

1. Landing Page Description (150-200 words): What the webinar covers, key takeaways (3-4 bullet points), speaker name and title, date/time, and registration CTA linking to [REGISTRATION URL].

2. Email Invitation: Email Body (100–150 words): Opener = 1 sentence naming the specific pain point (not the webinar title). Body = 2–3 sentences building urgency around the topic. CTA = 1 sentence (15–25 words): 'Register now at [URL] — [X] spots remaining / free to attend.' Subject line ≤ 60 characters, no 'webinar' in the subject (use the outcome instead). Sent to [TARGET AUDIENCE].

3. LinkedIn Post (100-150 words): Announcement post for organic reach targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Ends with 3-4 relevant hashtags including [BRAND HASHTAG].

4. Short Social Blurb (under 50 words): For Instagram or Twitter use.

Do not use em dashes in any asset. Avoid passive voice.

Webinar Details:
- Title: [INSERT TITLE]
- Speaker: [INSERT SPEAKER NAME AND TITLE]
- Date and Time: [INSERT DATE AND TIME]
- Registration URL: [INSERT URL]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B event / conversational community invite / urgency-led — default: professional B2B, consistent across all assets]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Asset Type | Content

Each 'Asset Type' row must be fully self-contained — no cross-references between rows.
Asset Type column must use exact labels: Email Subject, Email Body, LinkedIn Post, Twitter Post, LinkedIn Ad Copy.
Field encoding: wrap multi-line assets in double quotes.

Rows: Landing Page Description | Email Subject | Email Body | LinkedIn Post | Social Blurb

Save to output/Webinar_Promo/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- All 4 assets generated in one request
- Consistent message and tone across all assets
- Tone conveys urgency (seats limited, date approaching) without tipping into salesy pressure - lead with what the attendee gains, not with manufactured scarcity, and avoid banned CTA phrasing (see templates/_shared.py BANNED_CTA_PHRASES, e.g. "Don't miss out", "Discover")
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- Registration URL in every asset
- SELF-CHECK: After drafting each asset, verify word counts: Email subject line ≤ 60 characters, Email body 100–150 words, LinkedIn post 100–200 words, Social caption ≤ 150 characters. Revise any asset outside its range before outputting.
- Output saved to output/Webinar_Promo/
- The identical opening hook or closing CTA reused across multiple webinars promoted in the same session - if you are generating promo copy for more than one webinar in a session, no two webinars may share an opening hook or closing CTA

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Subject line containing the word 'webinar' — use the outcome promise instead (e.g., 'How to close skills gaps in 90 days' not 'Webinar: skills gap analysis').
2. Email body over 150 words due to speaker biography — the speaker bio belongs on the landing page, not in the email.
3. LinkedIn post and email using identical opening sentences — each asset must open differently.
4. CTA missing the specific URL or registration instruction — every asset must link to the registration page explicitly.
5. Asset types not covering all required channels — generate all five asset types in every request.
