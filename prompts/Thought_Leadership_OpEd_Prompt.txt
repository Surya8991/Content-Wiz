WHAT IS A THOUGHT LEADERSHIP OP-ED
------------------------------------------------------------

A thought leadership op-ed is a 600-900 word opinion piece written from the perspective of a senior executive (CEO, Founder, VP) sharing a strong, evidence-backed point of view on an industry topic. Unlike a blog post, it takes a clear stance, challenges conventional thinking, and positions the author as a credible voice in the industry. It is designed for owned channels (company blog, LinkedIn Article) and earned media (industry publications, guest posts).

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Write a thought leadership op-ed for [AUTHOR NAME], [AUTHOR TITLE] at [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], on the topic "[TOPIC]". The op-ed should take a clear, opinionated stance on "[POINT OF VIEW e.g. Why most L&D programmes fail / The real reason managers do not become leaders]". Open with a personal observation or bold contrarian claim. Build the argument with 2-3 evidence-backed paragraphs referencing credible research or real-world examples. Include one personal insight or anecdote that only this executive could offer. Close with a forward-looking statement and a CTA referencing [URL]. Write in first-person, conversational but authoritative tone. Do not use em dashes. Avoid passive voice. Do not use pronouns: you, we, our, your.

Point of View / Stance: [INSERT THE ARGUMENT OR OPINION]
Reference URL (if any): [INSERT URL]
Target Publication: [e.g. Company Blog / LinkedIn Article / Forbes / HR Magazine]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Sr. No. | Author | Topic | Point of View | Target Publication | Op-Ed Content

Save to output/Thought_Leadership/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Must take a clear, defensible stance - not neutral
- Opens with a personal observation or contrarian claim
- 600-900 words
- At least 2 credible stats or research references, each naming its source, organization, and year
- First-person throughout
- No pronouns: you, we, our, your
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- The identical opening claim or closing CTA reused across multiple op-eds in the same batch - if you are generating more than one row in a session, no two may share an opening observation or a closing statement
- Output saved to output/Thought_Leadership/
