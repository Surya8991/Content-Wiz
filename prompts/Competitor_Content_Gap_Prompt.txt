============================================================
COMPETITOR CONTENT GAP ANALYSIS PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Conduct a content gap analysis for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], against the following competitor(s): [COMPETITOR NAMES / URLS]. Research the content topics, formats, and keywords the competitors are covering that [BRAND NAME] is not. Identify gaps across blog content, FAQs, guides, and resource pages. For each gap identified, provide: the topic area, the competitor covering it, why it matters for [TARGET AUDIENCE], and a recommended content idea for [BRAND NAME] to fill the gap. Do not use em dashes.

Brand Website: [INSERT BRAND URL]
Competitor URLs:
1. [INSERT COMPETITOR 1 URL]
2. [INSERT COMPETITOR 2 URL]
3. [INSERT COMPETITOR 3 URL - OPTIONAL]

Focus Area (if any): [e.g. Leadership training / Agile certifications / HR topics]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B / conversational B2B / first-person / brand account - default: professional B2B]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Sr. No. | Topic Area | Competitor Covering It | Why It Matters | Recommended Content Idea for Brand | Suggested Format

Field encoding: wrap any field containing commas or line breaks in double quotes.
'Why It Matters': 1–2 sentences stating the audience need and business opportunity.
'Recommended Content Idea': name the format and the specific angle (e.g. 'Long-form guide: How to measure behavior change after compliance training').

Save to output/Competitor_Gap_Analysis/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Minimum 10 gap opportunities identified
- Each recommended content idea must name the content format, the specific angle, and the primary reader question it answers. Generic topic labels ('write about leadership') fail this test.
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- Research must be grounded in actual competitor content, not assumptions
- Any statistic or data point cited to justify why a gap matters must name its source, organization, and year. Never invent a plausible-sounding citation; if a claim cannot be verified, describe the gap qualitatively instead
- SELF-CHECK: Each 'Why It Matters' cell must be 1–2 sentences. Each 'Recommended Content Idea' must be 1–2 sentences with a named format. Revise any under-spec cell before outputting.
- BATCH DEDUPLICATION: When generating multiple gap analyses in one session, no two rows may recommend the same content format and angle combination; no two rows may cite the same competitor for different gaps without naming a different piece of their content. Vary angle and evidence across every item in the batch.
- Output saved to output/Competitor_Gap_Analysis/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. GAP IDENTIFIED THAT THE BRAND ALREADY COVERS: Before flagging a topic as a gap, verify the brand does not have an existing article, guide, or resource covering that angle. A false gap wastes production budget.

2. RECOMMENDED IDEA TOO BROAD TO COMMISSION DIRECTLY: "Write a guide on leadership training" is not a commissioning-ready brief. The recommended idea must name the format, the angle, and the specific reader question it answers.

3. COMPETITOR LISTED WITHOUT VERIFYING THEY ACTUALLY COVER THAT TOPIC: Do not infer that a competitor covers a topic because they operate in the same niche. Confirm the specific piece of content before citing it as the gap-filler benchmark.

4. 'WHY IT MATTERS' THAT RESTATES THE GAP WITHOUT EXPLAINING THE AUDIENCE NEED: "The brand does not cover this topic" is not a reason it matters. The cell must explain what the target audience needs from this topic and why the absence hurts the brand's authority or pipeline.
