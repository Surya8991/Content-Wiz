LINKEDIN CAROUSEL PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, provide all required inputs and generate the carousel.

------------------------------------------------------------
WHY CAROUSELS OUTPERFORM OTHER FORMATS
------------------------------------------------------------

LinkedIn carousels generate 1.45x average reach compared to standard text posts as of 2026. The swipe interaction signals active engagement to the algorithm, which treats each swipe as a positive engagement signal. A carousel that earns 8 swipes on 500 impressions outperforms a text post that earns 80 likes on 5,000 impressions for algorithmic distribution.

The rule: each slide must be worth swiping to. A viewer who stops swiping mid-carousel sends a negative signal. Every slide must either answer the implicit question raised by the previous slide or reward the viewer with a new, concrete piece of information.

------------------------------------------------------------
CAROUSEL TYPES - CHOOSE ONE
------------------------------------------------------------

TYPE 1: FRAMEWORK CAROUSEL
Structure: Slide 1 (hook claim) → Slide 2 (why it matters) → Slides 3-8 (one framework component per slide) → Slide 9 (how it connects) → Slide 10 (CTA)
Best for: Named frameworks with 3 to 7 components. Each component gets one slide.

TYPE 2: STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS
Structure: Slide 1 (the outcome the process achieves) → Slide 2 (what most people do wrong) → Slides 3-8 (one numbered step per slide) → Slide 9 (the result when done correctly) → Slide 10 (CTA)
Best for: How-to content with a clear sequence. Number the steps visibly on each slide.

TYPE 3: STAT BREAKDOWN
Structure: Slide 1 (the most surprising stat) → Slides 2-8 (one stat per slide with context) → Slide 9 (what these stats mean combined) → Slide 10 (CTA)
Best for: Research findings, original data, industry reports. Each slide: stat + source + one-sentence implication.

TYPE 4: BEFORE AND AFTER
Structure: Slide 1 (the gap between before and after) → Slides 2-7 (alternating before/after pairs on a specific dimension) → Slide 8 (the principle that explains the gap) → Slide 9 (CTA)
Best for: Case study highlights, methodology comparisons, transformation stories.

TYPE 5: MYTH VS FACT
Structure: Slide 1 (the most widely believed myth) → Slides 2-8 (one myth/fact pair per slide) → Slide 9 (the underlying principle) → Slide 10 (CTA)
Best for: Challenging conventional wisdom with evidence. Each myth must be one the target audience genuinely holds.

------------------------------------------------------------
SLIDE-BY-SLIDE STRUCTURE (UNIVERSAL)
------------------------------------------------------------

SLIDE 1: HOOK
This slide determines whether anyone swipes. It must work as a complete standalone claim.
- Headline: 5 to 8 words. The most counterintuitive or specific claim in the carousel.
- Subtext (optional): 10 to 15 words adding one specific detail that sharpens the hook.
- Visual: High contrast. Bold text dominant. A human face or a sharp visual tension element (chart gap, split comparison).
- Do not use: "X things you need to know about Y" as the hook. Lead with the claim, not the count.

Hook patterns:
  PATTERN 1: "[Specific number] [audience] [surprising finding]."
  PATTERN 2: "The [named problem] most [audience] never name."
  PATTERN 3: "[Common belief]. [Contradicting fact]."
  PATTERN 4: "[Outcome] in [timeframe]. Here is how."
  PATTERN 5: "Stop [common action]. Do [specific alternative] instead."

SLIDES 2 TO (N-1): CONTENT SLIDES
Each content slide follows this 3-element format:
  - HEADLINE (max 8 words): One complete idea. Reads as a standalone claim.
  - BODY COPY (max 25 words): One sentence of explanation or evidence. Named stat with source and year if applicable.
  - VISUAL DIRECTION: Specific description of what appears on screen (diagram, icon, before/after split, stat callout, progress indicator).

Rules for content slides:
  - One idea per slide. Never two.
  - Each slide must be readable in under 5 seconds.
  - Number framework steps or myth/fact pairs visibly (e.g., "Step 3" or "Myth 4").
  - If using a stat: state the source and year on the slide itself, not just in the caption.
  - Progress indicator (e.g., "4 of 9") in a corner of each slide improves swipe-through rate.

SECOND-TO-LAST SLIDE: THE SYNTHESIS
Before the CTA, include one slide that pulls the carousel together:
  - What does following this framework / believing these facts / completing these steps actually produce?
  - State the result as a specific outcome, not a vague benefit.
  - Example: "Organizations that complete these 5 steps reduce onboarding time by an average of 60%, according to 2026 Edstellar program data."

FINAL SLIDE: CTA
One ask only. Match to the carousel topic.
  - Headline: "Want the full [framework / guide / data]?" or "[Specific next step] is here."
  - Body: One sentence. The resource or next action at the destination URL.
  - Do not put the URL on the slide. URL goes in the post caption's first comment.
  - Visual: Brand colors, brand name, clean layout. Optional: QR code linking to the destination URL.

------------------------------------------------------------
POST CAPTION (GENERATED ALONGSIDE THE CAROUSEL)
------------------------------------------------------------

The carousel caption is short. The carousel does the heavy lifting.

Structure (follow Format B from LinkedIn_Post_Prompt.txt):
- Line 1: Repeat the exact Slide 1 headline (visual continuity when the image loads in feed)
- Lines 2 to 3: Two teaser lines - name two of the carousel's most surprising slides in one phrase each
- Line 4: "Swipe to see the full [framework / breakdown / process]." - no URL here
- Line 5: CTC question (one specific question inviting comments)
- Final line: 3 to 5 hashtags, last always [BRAND HASHTAG]
- First comment: URL + "Full [article / guide / case study] here: [URL]"

Character target: under 300 characters for the caption body (carousel earns the engagement, not the caption length).

------------------------------------------------------------
GLOBAL RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- Headline max 8 words per slide
- Body copy max 25 words per slide
- URL never on the slide - always in the first comment
- Every stat names source and year
- Progress indicator recommended on all slides 2 through final
- Output saved to output/LinkedIn_Carousel/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. SLIDE 1 AS A TITLE CARD: A first slide that just names the topic ("5 Ways to Improve Corporate Training") earns no swipes. The hook slide must make a claim strong enough to earn the first swipe on its own merit.

2. MORE THAN ONE IDEA PER SLIDE: A slide with two insights competes with itself. Viewers read the first idea and swipe before finishing the second. One idea, fully expressed, per slide.

3. STATS WITHOUT SOURCES ON THE SLIDE: A stat cited in the caption but not on the slide loses credibility - the viewer sees the claim without the evidence. Put source and year on the slide itself, even in small text.

4. CTA SLIDE WITH A URL IN THE BODY: LinkedIn suppresses reach when posts contain external URLs. The CTA slide should name the destination without a live link. The URL goes in the first comment of the caption.

5. NO SYNTHESIS SLIDE BEFORE THE CTA: A carousel that ends abruptly with a CTA without connecting the content leaves the viewer without a takeaway. The synthesis slide converts a sequence of facts into a single memorable conclusion.
