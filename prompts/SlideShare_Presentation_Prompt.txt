============================================================
SLIDESHARE / PRESENTATION SCRIPT PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Create a SlideShare presentation script for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], on the topic "[TOPIC]". Generate 10-12 slides. For each slide provide: a slide number, a short headline (under 10 words), 3-5 bullet points of concise supporting content, and a speaker note of 2-3 sentences expanding on the slide. The presentation should open with a compelling title slide, build through insight slides, and close with a CTA slide: Headline = 'What's Your Next Step?' (or equivalent). Bullets = 2–3 action options. Speaker Notes must include a closing sentence of 15–25 words naming the specific resource at [URL]. Target audience is [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Match the brand's market register per _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt rule 3 (default: clear, professional B2B tone), but keep it punchier and more visual than prose: each slide should read as a scannable takeaway a viewer can absorb in seconds, not a paragraph broken into lines. Save the fuller explanation for the speaker notes. Do not use em dashes. Avoid passive voice.

Reference URL (if any): [INSERT URL]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B / conversational B2B / first-person / brand account — default: professional B2B]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Slide No. | Slide Headline | Bullet Points | Speaker Notes

Field encoding: wrap multi-line cells in double quotes.
Bullet Points column: use a pipe | to separate individual bullet points within one CSV cell (e.g., 'Point 1 | Point 2 | Point 3').
Speaker Notes column: min 30 words per slide, written as spoken delivery script not a repeat of the bullet points.

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- 10-12 slides total
- Slide headline under 10 words
- 3-5 bullet points per slide, each under 15 words
- Speaker notes 2-3 sentences per slide, minimum 30 words
- First slide is title, last slide is summary and CTA
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- SELF-CHECK: After drafting, verify each slide: Headline ≤ 10 words, Bullets = 3–5 per slide. Count both. If any slide exceeds its headline limit or has more than 5 bullets, revise before outputting.
- BATCH DEDUPLICATION: When generating multiple presentations in one session, no two Slide 1 headlines may share the same opening word or structure. No two CTA slides may share the same headline or CTA sentence.
- Output saved to output/SlideShare/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Slide headline over 10 words — count the words and trim before outputting.
2. Bullet points that are full sentences instead of fragments — each bullet must be a concise phrase a viewer can read in 3 seconds.
3. Speaker notes that repeat the bullet points verbatim — notes must expand on the bullets, not restate them.
4. CTA slide missing a specific URL or next-step action — the final slide must name where to go and why.
5. No data or stat on slides 3–8 — at least one slide in the body must carry a named statistic with source and year.
