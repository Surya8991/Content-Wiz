============================================================
SKILLS GAP ANALYSIS REPORT PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Write a skills gap analysis report for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], for the industry or role "[INDUSTRY / ROLE / FUNCTION e.g. HR professionals in financial services / Project managers in tech companies]". The report should be 1200-1800 words and structured as follows: an executive summary, a market context section on why this skill gap matters now, identification of the top 5-8 skills gaps with evidence, the business impact of each gap, recommended training interventions for each gap, and a conclusion with a CTA linking to [URL]. Conclusion (80–120 words): state the 2 highest-priority gaps, the recommended first action, and a CTA of 15–25 words naming a specific next step. Example CTA: 'Start with a skills assessment workshop — [URL] to book a diagnostic session.' Include at least 5 credible statistics or research references. Write in an authoritative, data-led tone targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE e.g. L&Ds, HRs, and decision-makers]. Do not use em dashes. Avoid passive voice. Naturally position [BRAND NAME] as the training partner that closes these gaps without being promotional.

Industry / Role Focus: [INSERT e.g. HR professionals / Sales teams / Project managers]
Reference URL: [INSERT URL]
Training Solutions to Reference (optional): [INSERT COURSE NAMES OR LEAVE BLANK]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B / conversational B2B / first-person / brand account — default: professional B2B]

------------------------------------------------------------
REPORT STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Report Title
2. Executive Summary (100-150 words)
3. Why This Skills Gap Matters Now (market context with data)
4. Skills Gap 1: Name | Evidence | Business Impact | Recommended Intervention
5. Skills Gap 2: Name | Evidence | Business Impact | Recommended Intervention
6. Skills Gap 3: Name | Evidence | Business Impact | Recommended Intervention
7. Skills Gap 4: Name | Evidence | Business Impact | Recommended Intervention
8. Skills Gap 5: Name | Evidence | Business Impact | Recommended Intervention
9. (Optional gaps 6-8)
10. Conclusion with CTA
11. About [BRAND NAME]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Skills Gap No. | Skill Name | Evidence / Stat | Business Impact | Recommended Training Intervention

Field encoding: wrap multi-line cells in double quotes.
Each row in the CSV must cover one discrete skill gap (not a department-level theme).
Recommended Intervention column: name the specific training format + delivery method + estimated time-to-competency.

Full report saved as plain text to output/Skills_Gap_Reports/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Minimum 5 skills gaps identified
- Each gap must include evidence, business impact, and a training recommendation
- Minimum 5 credible stats, each naming the source, organization, and year - never "studies show" or "research suggests" without attribution
- Brand positioned as training solution, not vendor pitch
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- SELF-CHECK: After drafting, count words. Target: 1,200–1,800. If under 1,200, expand the Recommended Interventions section. If over 1,800, trim the background context paragraph.
- BATCH DEDUPLICATION: When generating multiple skills gap analyses in one session, no two reports may share the same top-priority gap, the same opening data point, or the same CTA sentence. Vary the leading finding and the recommended first action.
- Output saved to output/Skills_Gap_Reports/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Gap identified at department level instead of skill level — each row must name a discrete, trainable skill, not a broad function like 'communication'.
2. Intervention named without a delivery method or time estimate — 'leadership training' fails; 'a 3-day in-person facilitation workshop, 4–6 weeks to competency' passes.
3. Word count under 1,200 due to skipped Evidence section — every gap must carry named evidence, not just an assertion.
4. CTA that promotes a product feature instead of a specific diagnostic action — close with an actionable next step (e.g., a skills assessment) not a product page link.
