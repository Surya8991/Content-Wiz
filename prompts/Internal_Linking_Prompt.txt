============================================================
INTERNAL LINKING SUGGESTIONS PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Generate internal linking suggestions for [BRAND NAME] for the blog post titled "[BLOG TITLE]" at [TARGET URL]. Given the list of existing blog URLs provided below, identify the most relevant pages to link to from the target post and the most relevant pages that should link back to the target post. For each suggestion provide: the source page, the anchor text recommendation, the destination URL, and the reason for the link. Do not use em dashes.

Target Blog URL: [INSERT TARGET URL]
Existing Blog URLs to consider:
[PASTE LIST OF EXISTING BLOG URLS]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Link Direction | Source Page Title | Source URL | Anchor Text | Destination URL | Reason

Link Direction options: Outbound (from target post) / Inbound (to target post)

Save to output/Internal_Linking/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Anchor text must be natural and contextually relevant, not generic ("click here", "read more"). Examples of natural anchor text (write new ones based on the actual page content, do not copy verbatim): "our skills gap analysis framework", "how we measure training ROI", "the complete guide to L&D budgeting", "see how enterprise teams onboard faster"
- Minimum 3 outbound and 3 inbound suggestions
- No two anchor text suggestions in the same output may be identical or near-identical phrasing
- Reason column must explain the topical relevance
- No em dashes anywhere
- Output saved to output/Internal_Linking/
