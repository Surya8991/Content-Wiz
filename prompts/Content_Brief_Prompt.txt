============================================================
CONTENT BRIEF PROMPT (WITH GEO / AEO FIELDS)
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Create a detailed content brief for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], for a [CONTENT TYPE e.g. blog post / pillar page / landing page] on the topic "[TOPIC]". The brief must be detailed enough for a writer to execute without any additional briefing. Target audience is [TARGET AUDIENCE]. Do not use em dashes. Avoid passive voice.

Reference URL (if any): [INSERT URL]
Target Platform: [e.g. WordPress / Medium / Dev.to]
Primary Target Keyword: [INSERT OR LEAVE BLANK]
Voice/Tone: [formal B2B / conversational B2B / first-person / brand account - default: professional B2B]

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT (CSV)
------------------------------------------------------------

Field | Detail

STANDARD SEO FIELDS:
- Content Goal
- Target Audience
- Primary Keyword
- Secondary Keywords (comma separated)
- Suggested Titles (3 options)
- Recommended Word Count
- Content Outline (H1 / H2s / H3s)
- Key Points to Cover: list 5–8 specific points, each as a complete sentence stating the point and why it matters to the reader
- Stats or Research to Include (name source, organization, and year for each)
- Internal Linking Suggestions (page title and URL)
- CTA Recommendation: one sentence, imperative verb, names a specific resource or action (e.g. 'Download the L&D ROI Calculator' or 'Book a free training audit'). Max 20 words.
- Competitor Reference URLs

GEO / AEO FIELDS (NEW):
- Primary Target Query (the exact question this content should answer for AI tools and featured snippets)
- Featured Snippet Answer (40-60 word direct answer to the target query, written to be pulled verbatim)
- People Also Ask Questions (4 related questions this content should also answer)
- AI Overview Optimisation Note (which claim or stat is most likely to be cited by an AI Overview and why)
- GEO Readiness Checklist:
    [ ] Does the introduction directly answer the target query in 2-3 sentences?
    [ ] Are all stats attributed to a named source, organization, and year?
    [ ] Are all key entities clearly named and defined?
    [ ] Are paragraphs 3-4 sentences or shorter?
    [ ] Is there a FAQ section with 4-6 direct-answer Q&As?

Save to output/Content_Brief/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Brief must be detailed enough for a writer to execute without additional briefing
- Primary keyword must appear naturally in title and at least 2 subheadings
- GEO / AEO fields are mandatory for all blog and pillar page briefs
- Featured snippet answer must be 40-60 words, direct, and quotable
- All stats must name the source, organization, and year
- Never invent a plausible-sounding source or statistic; if a claim cannot be verified, flag it for verification or recommend a qualitative statement instead
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- SELF-CHECK: Featured snippet answer must be 40–60 words. Count after drafting and trim or expand if outside range.
- BATCH DEDUPLICATION: When generating multiple briefs in one session, no two may share the same suggested title structure, the same featured snippet opening phrase, or the same CTA action. Vary angle and evidence across every item in the batch.
- Output saved to output/Content_Brief/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. PRIMARY KEYWORD NOT NATURALLY MATCHING THE STATED SEARCH INTENT: A keyword like "corporate training" assigned to a decision-stage brief targeting buyers ready to book a demo is misaligned. The keyword and the content goal must reflect the same stage of the buyer journey.

2. FEATURED SNIPPET ANSWER OVER 60 WORDS: Google's featured snippet window is 40–60 words. An answer that runs to 90 words will be cut or skipped. Trim to the precise answer without preamble.

3. CTA NOT TIED TO THE ARTICLE TOPIC: A CTA for a demo page on an awareness-stage article about measurement frameworks creates friction. The CTA must match the content type and the buyer's stage, as defined in the Content Goal field.

4. KEY POINTS LISTED AS TOPIC LABELS NOT READER-BENEFIT STATEMENTS: "Cover ROI" is a topic label. "Explain why completion rate is a lagging indicator and what leading indicators to track instead" is a reader-benefit statement. Every key point must tell the writer what the reader should understand after reading it.
