============================================================
ORIGINAL RESEARCH / SURVEY REPORT PROMPT
============================================================

BRAND DETECTION: Follow _Brand_Detection_Rules.txt before generating.

Once the brand is confirmed, use the template below.

------------------------------------------------------------
TEMPLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Write an original research report for [BRAND NAME], [BRAND DESCRIPTION], titled "[REPORT TITLE]" based on the following data or survey findings: [PASTE DATA / SURVEY RESULTS OR DESCRIBE THE DATASET]. The report should be 1500-2000 words. Structure it with an executive summary, methodology note, 4-5 key findings sections each anchored by a headline stat, analysis and interpretation of each finding, implications for [TARGET AUDIENCE], and a conclusion with recommendations and CTA linking to [URL]. Write in an authoritative, analytical tone. Position [BRAND NAME] as the source of the research naturally. Every headline stat and supporting figure must name its source, organization, and year: if the figure comes from the brand's own survey or program data, cite it as "[BRAND NAME]'s [Year] [survey/program name]"; never present an unverified or first-party number as fact without naming where it came from. Do not use em dashes. Avoid passive voice.

Data or Survey Input: [PASTE FINDINGS OR DESCRIBE DATASET]
Report URL (if published): [INSERT URL]
Research Focus Area: [e.g. L&D investment trends / Workforce skills gaps / HR priorities]
Voice/Tone: [authoritative / analytical / accessible B2B — default: authoritative, analytical, third-person]

------------------------------------------------------------
REPORT STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Report Title and Subtitle
2. Executive Summary (150-200 words, 3-5 headline stats)
3. Methodology (sample size, data source, date range)
4. Key Finding 1 (headline stat + analysis + implication)
5. Key Finding 2 (headline stat + analysis + implication)
6. Key Finding 3 (headline stat + analysis + implication)
7. Key Finding 4 (headline stat + analysis + implication)
8. Key Finding 5 (optional)
9. Recommendations for [TARGET AUDIENCE]
10. Conclusion (100–150 words): summarise 2–3 key findings, state what the reader should do differently, close with a CTA of 15–25 words naming a specific resource.
11. About [BRAND NAME] (50–80 words): company descriptor, one proof point, one contact or URL.

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT FORMAT
------------------------------------------------------------

Full report in plain text with clearly labelled sections.

Heading format: use ALL CAPS for section titles. Subsections use Title Case. Each section must be separated by a blank line.

Conclusion section: 100–150 words maximum.

About [BRAND NAME] section: 50–80 words, no product claims.

Save to output/Research_Reports/

------------------------------------------------------------
RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Executive summary must include 3-5 headline stats
- Each finding must include a headline stat, 2-3 sentences of analysis, and 1-2 sentences on implications
- Every stat in the report names its source, organization, and year, including first-party/brand data (cite it as the brand's own survey or dataset, do not present it as an unattributed fact)
- Never state a figure as fact if it cannot be traced to the pasted data or a named, real source; flag anything unverifiable rather than inventing a precise-sounding number
- Methodology section is mandatory even if brief
- No em dashes anywhere
- No passive voice
- Brand positioned as research source, not as vendor
- SELF-CHECK: After drafting, count total words. Target: 1,500–2,000. If under 1,500, expand the Methodology or Key Findings sections. If over 2,000, trim the Introduction and Limitations sections first.
- BATCH DEDUPLICATION: When generating multiple research reports in one session, no two may share the same executive summary opening sentence, the same primary data source, or the same CTA action. Vary the central finding and the evidence anchor across reports.
- Output saved to output/Research_Reports/

------------------------------------------------------------
FAILURE MODES TO AVOID
------------------------------------------------------------

1. METHODOLOGY SECTION MISSING SAMPLE SIZE AND DATA SOURCE: A methodology note that says "research was conducted" without naming the sample size, data source, and date range is unverifiable and undermines the report's credibility. Always include these three elements even if brief.

2. FINDINGS STATED AS OPINIONS WITHOUT SUPPORTING DATA: Every key finding section must be anchored by a headline stat with a named source. A finding presented as a general observation without data is an opinion, not research.

3. CTA IN CONCLUSION THAT PROMOTES A PRODUCT INSTEAD OF A RELATED RESOURCE: The conclusion CTA should link to a resource (framework, guide, assessment) that extends the report's value, not to a product demo or sales page.

4. WORD COUNT UNDER 1,500 DUE TO SKIPPED LIMITATIONS SECTION: Omitting the Limitations section is a common shortcut that also signals to readers that the research is not rigorous. Include it even if brief (2–3 sentences).

5. ABOUT SECTION OVER 80 WORDS WITH SALES COPY: The About section should describe what the brand does and provide one proof point. Any language that promotes a product feature or makes an unsubstantiated claim must be removed.
